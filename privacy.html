Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 8, 2026

The short version: Somedays is a local-first app. Your data lives on your device. We don't track you, we don't run analytics, and we don't sell data to anyone.

What stays on your device

Your saved places, collections, trips, and usage data are stored locally on your iPhone. If you never sign in, nothing ever leaves your device.

What we process on our server

When you paste a link to extract places, that URL is sent to our server for processing. We use it to pull out place information and then discard the request. We don't log or store the URLs you submit.

Cloud sync (optional, Pro only)

If you sign in with Apple and have a Pro subscription, your places and collections sync to our cloud database (Supabase) so you can access them on other devices. This data is tied to an anonymous identifier from Apple — not your name or email.

Sign in with Apple

We use Sign in with Apple for authentication. Apple gives us an anonymous user ID. We don't receive your Apple ID password, and we only get your email if you choose to share it.

Payments

Subscriptions are handled entirely by Apple through the App Store. We never see or store your payment information.

Third-party services

When enriching place data, we send place names to the Google Places API to get ratings and photos. Google's own privacy policy governs how they handle that data.

What we don't do

  • We don't use analytics or tracking SDKs.
  • We don't collect advertising identifiers.
  • We don't sell or share your data with anyone.
  • We don't access your location.

Deleting your data

You can delete all local data from Settings inside the app. You can also export everything as JSON first. If you've synced to the cloud, contact us and we'll delete your cloud data too.

Children

Somedays is not directed at children under 13.

Changes

If we update this policy, the date at the top will change. That's it.
